# v0.3 stateful workflow preview

DecisionAgentBench v0.3.0 adds a separate dependency-enforced evaluation suite. It does not rewrite
the frozen v0.1 task families or the v0.2.1 seeded expansion.

## Exact scope

The suite contains **3 workflow concepts, 12 seeded instances, and 24 paired samples**: 12 clean
samples and their 12 stressed counterparts. The concepts cover a regional demand turnaround, a
vendor-product pilot, and recall containment and recovery. Four deterministic seeds vary event
payloads without changing the workflow contract.

Every sample requires 20 persisted transitions over at least 15 simulated days. Transitions are
checked against prerequisites, minimum simulated time, delayed-event arrival, and evidence from
specified earlier tools. The final transition also depends directly on the first, producing a
target dependency span of 19. An invalid transition is denied and remains in the trace.

## Stateful execution

Four v0.3 tools expose the workflow boundary:

- `inspect_workflow` reports current state, transition gates, and revealed events;
- `execute_workflow_step` attempts one transition with cited prior evidence;
- `advance_workflow_time` moves the deterministic clock and applies due events; and
- `rollback_workflow_step` reverses the specifically required mutable transition.

The workflow state is stored in the per-sample SQLite world. Regional price pilots, vendor pilot
inventory, substitute inventory, and recall closure modify ordinary simulator tables. Reversible
changes record before and after values in a private mutation ledger. Workflow tables are not
available through the agent's read-only retail SQL interface.

At days 5, 10, and 15, delayed checkpoints become observable. Stressed samples also reveal a
workflow-specific disruption at day 10 and block later transitions until step `S08` is rolled back.
Clean and stressed samples otherwise share the same 20-step graph.

## Trace-derived grading

Task effectiveness comes from executed transition coverage, not answer keywords. Decision quality
comes from a deterministic workflow outcome combining completion, dependency integrity, temporal
integrity, and required recovery. The scorer publishes:

- completed and required transition counts;
- measured dependency span;
- simulated days;
- invalid transition attempts;
- applied delayed events;
- rollback count and recovery status; and
- a stable terminal-state digest.

The v0.2.1 evidence eligibility rule remains active. A fluent final answer cannot compensate for an
unexecuted workflow or unsupported evidence, and incomplete or invalid chains receive `F-PLAN`.

## Claim boundary

v0.3.0 is a **dependency-enforced horizon preview**, not a claim of parity with RetailBench,
YC-Bench, LongDS-Bench, or METR's human-time methodology. It demonstrates executable temporal
structure and trace measurement in a small, synthetic suite. It does not yet provide a skilled-
human completion-time study, frontier-model results, or an external reproduction. See
[horizon methodology](horizon-methodology.md) for the comparison and acceptance criteria.
